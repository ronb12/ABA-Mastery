[SLIDE 1 - TITLE]
"Hi everyone! Today we're covering Backward Chaining - an important concept for the BCBA and BCaBA certification exams."

[SLIDE 2 - THE QUESTION]
"Let's start with a sample exam question:
In backward chaining, which step of the task is taught FIRST?"

[SLIDE 3 - ANSWER OPTIONS]
"Your options are:
A. The first step
B. The middle step
C. The last step
D. All steps simultaneously"

[SLIDE 4 - CORRECT ANSWER]
"The correct answer is C: The last step"

[SLIDE 5 - DETAILED EXPLANATION]
"Let me explain why this is correct:

BACKWARD CHAINING teaches the LAST step first. Instructor completes all steps except the final one, learner completes final step and gets natural reinforcement (task completion). Then teach second-to-last step, etc., working backward. Advantage: learner always experiences task completion/natural reinforcement. FORWARD chaining teaches first step first, working forward."

[SLIDE 6 - COMMON MISTAKES]
"Here's why the other options are incorrect:

- The middle step: This is incorrect because...
- All steps simultaneously: This is incorrect because..."

"The key takeaway: Remember this distinction, and you'll be able to answer any exam question on this topic."
