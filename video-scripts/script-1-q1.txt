[SLIDE 1 - TITLE]
"Hi everyone! Today we're covering Positive vs Negative Reinforcement - an important concept for the BCBA and BCaBA certification exams."

[SLIDE 2 - THE QUESTION]
"Let's start with a sample exam question:
Which of the following best describes positive reinforcement?"

[SLIDE 3 - ANSWER OPTIONS]
"Your options are:
A. Removing an aversive stimulus following a behavior
B. Adding a stimulus following a behavior that increases future probability of that behavior
C. Adding an aversive stimulus following a behavior to decrease it
D. Withholding reinforcement to decrease behavior"

[SLIDE 4 - CORRECT ANSWER]
"The correct answer is B: Adding a stimulus following a behavior that increases future probability of that behavior"

[SLIDE 5 - DETAILED EXPLANATION]
"Let me explain why this is correct:

Positive reinforcement involves adding (positive) a stimulus following a behavior that increases (reinforcement) the future probability of that behavior occurring. The 'positive' refers to adding something, and 'reinforcement' means the behavior increases."

[SLIDE 6 - COMMON MISTAKES]
"Here's why the other options are incorrect:
- Removing an aversive stimulus following a behavior: This is incorrect because...
- Adding an aversive stimulus following a behavior to decrease it: This is incorrect because...
- Withholding reinforcement to decrease behavior: This is incorrect because..."

[SLIDE 7 - KEY TAKEAWAY]
"The key takeaway: Remember this distinction, and you'll be able to answer any exam question on this topic."
