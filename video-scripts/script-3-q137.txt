[SLIDE 1 - TITLE]
"Hi everyone! Today we're covering Selectionism and Behavior - an important concept for the BCBA and BCaBA certification exams."

[SLIDE 2 - THE QUESTION]
"Let's start with a sample exam question:
According to the principle of selectionism, behavior is selected by which three levels?"

[SLIDE 3 - ANSWER OPTIONS]
"Your options are:
A. Genetic, cultural, and social
B. Phylogenic, ontogenic, and cultural
C. Biological, psychological, and environmental
D. Natural, artificial, and automatic"

[SLIDE 4 - CORRECT ANSWER]
"The correct answer is B: Phylogenic, ontogenic, and cultural"

[SLIDE 5 - DETAILED EXPLANATION]
"Let me explain why this is correct:

Skinner's selectionism proposes three levels: (1) Phylogenic - genetic/evolutionary selection (species survival), (2) Ontogenic - selection through individual's lifetime (operant conditioning), and (3) Cultural - selection of practices by groups (cultural evolution). This parallels Darwin's natural selection."

"Here's why the other options are incorrect:

- Biological, psychological, and environmental: This is incorrect because...
- Natural, artificial, and automatic: This is incorrect because..."

"The key takeaway: Remember this distinction, and you'll be able to answer any exam question on this topic."
